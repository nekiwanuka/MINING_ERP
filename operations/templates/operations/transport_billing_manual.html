Workflow Manual

Shared vehicle customer billing

Use this process when one transit carries cargo for more than one customer.

1. Create one transit record

Create a transport record for the vehicle trip. The system generates one unique transit number. All cargo, route points, fees, and invoices stay attached to that transit.

2. Add customer cargo rows

For every customer sharing the vehicle, record the customer name, purchase order, cargo description, package details, weight or CBM, loading point, offloading point, and direct customer charges.

3. Mark route sequence

Use loading sequence, offloading sequence, and fee route sequence to show when cargo is onboard. A fee at sequence 3 only applies to customers whose cargo has loaded by sequence 3 and has not offloaded before it.

4. Add transit and government fees

Enter each border, toll, customs, duty, permit, tax, or checkpoint fee as a separate fee row. Include the place, sequence, reference, category, fee name, and amount.

5. Generate customer invoices

Open the transport detail page and click Generate invoices. The system creates one draft invoice for each customer cargo row.

6. How billing is calculated

Direct customer charges go fully to that customer. Shared fleet charges are allocated by chargeable units multiplied by billable distance. Transit and government fees are allocated only among customers onboard at the fee sequence.

7. Review before sending

Open each generated invoice and confirm the line items. The invoice explains the direct charges, shared fleet allocation, and transit/government fees for that customer.
