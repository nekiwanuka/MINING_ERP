Workflow Manual

One transit, multiple client billing

Use this process when one vehicle transit carries cargo for more than one client and each client is billed by their own route segment.

1. Create one transit record

Create one transport record for the vehicle trip. The system generates one unique transit number. Record the origin, final destination, transit start KM, common route end KM, and final destination KM so the system can separate the common route from the distribution route.

2. Add client cargo rows

For every client sharing the vehicle, record the client name, cargo description, loading point, offloading point, delivery point, delivery KM, rate per km, loading fee, offloading fee, document charge, and any approved custom client charge.

3. Calculate client-facing charges

The standard transport charge is rate per KM multiplied by the customer distance from transit start KM to delivery KM. Loading and offloading are charged only to the affected customer.

4. Enter planned shared transit costs

Fuel, driver allowance, turn boy allowance, vehicle operating allowance, planned road tolls, planned ferry fees, and planned miscellaneous costs are allocated by route occupancy. The common route is shared by all customers; distribution segments are shared only by customers still onboard.

5. Record transit point and journey costs

As the transit moves, enter each border tax, customs fee, road toll, emergency expense, or other journey cost with its date, time, KM location, transit point, cost type, amount, and notes. KM-based costs are shared only by customers still onboard at that KM.

6. Generate customer invoices

Invoices include the transport charge, customer-specific loading and offloading fees, allocated common route costs, allocated distribution route costs, and allocated transit point costs.

7. Track revenue

Transit revenue is the sum of all customer invoices for the transit.

8. Track profit or loss

The transport detail page compares invoice revenue against planned and recorded transit expenses. Profit is total customer revenue minus total transit expenses.
